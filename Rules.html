Welcome to the Badminton Rules

Badminton is governed by a comprehensive set of rules designed to ensure fair play and consistency in competitions. These rules are established by the Badminton World Federation (BWF), the global governing body of the sport. Here's an overview of the key rules in badminton:

Learn More

Court Dimensions and Equipment


    -Court Size: A badminton court is rectangular, measuring 13.4 meters (44 feet) long and 6.1 meters (20 feet) wide for doubles matches. For singles matches, the width is reduced to 5.18 meters (17 feet).

    -Net: The net is placed at the center, 1.55 meters (5 feet 1 inch) high at the edges and 1.524 meters (5 feet) high in the middle.

    -Shuttlecock: The shuttlecock, also known as the "birdie," is made of 16 feathers and weighs between 4.74 and 5.50 grams.

    -Racket: Badminton rackets are lightweight, usually weighing between 70 to 95 grams, excluding string.

Badminton Court Rules

A badminton court is a rectangular playing area with specific measurements. It's divided into two halves by a net in the middle. The court has different boundary lines depending on whether you're playing singles or doubles:


-Singles court: Narrower, with side lines closer to the center.

-Doubles court: Wider, with the outermost side lines used.

Key areas of the court include:

-Service courts: Two small boxes on each side of the net where players must serve.

-Backcourt: The rear area of each half, used during rallies and for defensive shots.

-Net area: Close to the net, good for drop shots and net play.

1. How to Play Badminton

-Serving

The game starts with a serve. The player or team serving hits the shuttlecock diagonally to the opponent’s side of the court.


For singles: If your score is even, you serve from the right side; if it’s odd, you serve from the left side.


For doubles: You always start serving from the right-hand service court, and the service rotates between players on the same team.


How to Serve: Hold the shuttlecock by the feathers, let it drop slightly, and hit it with an upward swing from below your waist. The shuttlecock must go over the net and land inside the other team’s service court.

-Playing the Rally

-After the serve, both players or teams hit the shuttlecock back and forth across the net.


-The goal is to make the shuttlecock land inside your opponent's court while preventing it from landing in yours.


-You can hit the shuttlecock once on your side before it must go over the net.

-Winning a Point

You win a point if:

-The shuttlecock lands in your opponent’s court and they don’t hit it back.

-Your opponent hits the shuttlecock out of bounds (outside the court lines).

-Your opponent hits the shuttlecock into the net.

-Your opponent makes a mistake like touching the net with their racket or body.

2. The Goal of Badminton

In badminton, the goal is to hit the shuttlecock (also called a birdie) over the net into your opponent’s court. If they can’t return it, you win a point! You play until one player or team wins two games out of three, and each game is played to 21 points.

3. The Equipment

    -Racket: This is what you use to hit the shuttlecock. It’s lightweight and has a long handle.
    -Shuttlecock: This is the small object with feathers (or plastic) that you hit back and forth. It’s also called a birdie.
    -Net: The net divides the court in half. You need to hit the shuttlecock over it.
    -Court: The court has two sides, and you play on opposite sides of the net. You can play either singles (1 vs. 1) or doubles (2 vs. 2).
