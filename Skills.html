SKILLS OF BADMINTON

When learning badminton, developing the right skills is key to becoming a better player. Badminton involves a combination of physical fitness, technique, and strategy. Here’s a breakdown of the essential skills for someone who’s new to badminton:...

Learn More

1. Grip

The way you hold the racket (your grip) is one of the most important skills in badminton because it affects how you hit the shuttlecock. There are two main types of grips:



-Forehand Grip: Used for most shots, especially when the shuttle is on your dominant side. Imagine shaking hands with the racket’s handle, with your thumb on one side and fingers wrapped around the other. This grip allows you to generate power and control.

-Backhand Grip: Used when the shuttle is on your non-dominant side. Rotate the racket slightly so your thumb is pressed against the back of the handle. This gives you better control and power for backhand shots.



Practice: Switching between the forehand and backhand grips will help you become comfortable no matter where the shuttle is on the court.

2. Footwork

Good footwork allows you to move quickly and efficiently around the court to reach the shuttlecock. Here are the key elements:



-Positioning: Always return to the center of the court after every shot. This is called the "base position" and it gives you the best chance to reach the next shot.

-Movement: Learn how to move forward, backward, and sideways without crossing your legs. Use small, quick steps (called "shuffle steps") to move efficiently.

-Balance: Stay low and balanced as you move, with your knees bent and your weight on the balls of your feet. This helps you react quickly to your opponent's shots.



Practice: Work on your footwork by moving to different spots on the court and returning to your base position after every shot.

3. Serving

Serving is how you start a rally, so mastering different serves is essential. There are two main types of serves in badminton:



Low Serve: The shuttlecock is hit softly and close to the net. The goal is to make it hard for your opponent to attack. This serve is usually used in doubles or for a defensive start.

High Serve: The shuttlecock is hit high and deep into your opponent’s court. This serve is commonly used in singles to push your opponent back.

How to Serve:

    -Stand sideways with one foot in front of the other.
    -Hold the shuttlecock by the feathers with your non-dominant hand.
    -Drop the shuttle and hit it with an underhand motion from below your waist.
    -Practice hitting the shuttle diagonally into your opponent’s service court.

4. Strokes (Types of Shots)

There are various types of shots in badminton, and learning them is crucial to becoming a good player.



Practice: Focus on controlling your shots. Start with the clear, then move on to the drop shot and smash. Practice each shot in different situations (forehand and backhand) to build versatility.

Here are the key shots to master:

-Clear: A high, deep shot that pushes your opponent to the back of the court. This shot gives you time to recover and forces your opponent to move.

-Drop Shot: A soft, controlled shot that just clears the net and lands near the front of your opponent’s court. It’s a great shot to use when your opponent is far back.

-Smash: A powerful, downward shot aimed at the ground on your opponent’s side. This is the most aggressive shot and is often used to finish a rally.

-Drive: A flat, fast shot that travels parallel to the floor. It’s typically used in doubles to keep the shuttle moving quickly and low across the court.

-Lift: A defensive shot that sends the shuttle high and deep into your opponent’s court. It’s used to buy time and force your opponent to the back of the court.
